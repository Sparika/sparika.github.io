With the advent of technology such as WebRTC, real-time communications are moving to the open Internet. This disruptive change will leads to the emergence of several new, unknown and even invisible actors participating in the establishment of a call session. For an end-user, trust in the system becomes blurred and difficult to understand.

We propose a method for modeling and computing the trust that an end-user (or a policy authority) could have in a given online communication setup. To this end, we build a user-centric functional decomposition of security properties and protocols from a communication setup. We use information-theory entropy as a base trust metric and compute a global trust value with aggregation operators. This end-value is then returned to the user.

This survey will be used to evaluate the interest, usefulness, and clarity of our model as a security knowledge representation.

The first half of the survey is dedicated to your understanding and point-of-view on security issues on the web and more precisely related to real time communications. The second half of the survey presents our framework, let you play with it on concrete examples, before asking questions regarding your perception the way information is represented.
